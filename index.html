Section 02

The World Comes to Malaysia

International arrivals hit a historic high of 27.4 million in 2014. The pandemic brought arrivals to near zero in 2021, but a strong recovery has Malaysia back to 25 million visitors in 2024 - close to its pre-pandemic peak.

International Arrivals to Malaysia (2014-2024)
Source: Tourism Malaysia via roadgenius.com (2025). Arrivals collapsed by 97% in 2021 due to COVID-19 border closures. Recovery accelerated after borders fully reopened in April 2022.
Top 10 Countries of Origin (2024, Million Visitors)
Source: Tourism Malaysia (Reuters, March 2026). Singapore alone accounts for 36% of all international arrivals, driven by geographic proximity and frequent cross-border travel.
Section 03

Where Do They Go?

Selangor, Kuala Lumpur, and Johor dominate Malaysia's domestic tourism in both visitor numbers and receipts. East Malaysian states like Sabah and Sarawak attract fewer domestic visitors, but those who visit tend to stay longer, reflecting their eco-tourism and nature-travel appeal.

Domestic Visitor Arrivals by State (2024, Millions)
Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey (States) 2024 (dosm.gov.my). Hover over a state to see visitor numbers. Map data: geoBoundaries (CC BY 4.0). Peninsular Malaysia's western corridor leads in domestic visitor volume.
Domestic Tourism Receipts by State (RM Billion, 2024)
Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey (States) 2024. Selangor (RM 14.2B) and W.P. Kuala Lumpur (RM 14.1B) together account for over RM 28 billion - more than a quarter of total domestic receipts of RM 106.7B.
Section 04

When Do They Visit?

Arrivals are not evenly spread across the year. December consistently records the highest monthly volumes, driven by year-end holidays and festive celebrations. August and June also peak, aligned with Malaysia's national celebrations and mid-year school breaks.

Monthly International Arrivals Heatmap (2022-2024, Million Visitors)
Source: Tourism Malaysia monthly statistics (roadgenius.com, 2025). Darker cells indicate higher visitor volumes. 2022 figures reflect partial-year recovery following border reopening in April 2022. December 2024 (2.54M) was the single highest monthly figure on record.
Section 05

What Do They Spend On?

Shopping is the dominant spending category at 37.4% of all domestic tourism expenditure, followed by food and beverage and accommodation. States like Sabah and Sarawak see longer average stays - translating to higher per-visitor economic value.

Domestic Tourism Spending Breakdown (2024)
Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey 2024. Total domestic expenditure: RM 106.7 billion. Shopping (37.4%), Food & Beverage (16.2%), Accommodation (~16.0%), Automotive Fuel (12.7%), Others (~17.7%).
Average Length of Stay by State (Nights, 2024)
Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey (States) 2024. National average is 2.49 nights (dashed line). Sabah and Sarawak lead due to their remote eco-tourism and adventure-travel destinations.
Section 06

Stay Longer, Spend More

States that attract longer-staying visitors tend to generate higher receipts per visitor. This scatter plot reveals two distinct clusters: high-volume, shorter-stay urban states like Selangor and KL versus lower-volume, longer-stay nature destinations like Sabah and Sarawak.

Length of Stay vs. Tourism Receipts by State (2024)
Source: DOSM Domestic Tourism Survey (States) 2024. Bubble size represents domestic visitor arrivals. States above the trend line generate above-average receipts relative to their length of stay.
Section 07

The Road to Recovery

From near zero in 2021, Malaysia's tourism staged a strong recovery. Key policy moves - reopening borders, visa-free access for China and India, and the KL20 summit - each accelerated the recovery curve toward pre-pandemic levels.

Section 08

What's Next? Visit Malaysia 2026

Malaysia has set an ambitious target of 27.3 million international visitors for 2026, backed by a RM 350 million government promotional budget. While 2024 fell short at 25 million, a 24.2% year-on-year increase shows the trajectory is upward.

Visitor Arrivals: 2019 Peak vs 2024 Actual vs 2026 Target (Millions)
Source: Tourism Malaysia; Reuters (March 2026); Malay Mail (February 2024). Malaysia needs 2.28M more visitors to reach its 2026 target - a 9.1% increase from the 2024 figure.

The Visit Malaysia 2026 Vision

The government campaign aims to position Malaysia as one of Southeast Asia's top travel destinations. Backed by ASEAN Chairmanship in 2025, expanded airline connectivity with Gulf and East Asian carriers, and ongoing visa liberalisation, Malaysia's tourism industry - which employs 3.5 million people - is aiming for record visitor numbers.
